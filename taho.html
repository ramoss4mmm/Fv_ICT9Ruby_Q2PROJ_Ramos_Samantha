Taho

Sources

Ingredients:

1 cup water

1 cup dark brown sugar

1 pandan leaf (tied into a knot)

1/2cup sago pearls

2 packages (12 ounces each) soft silken tofu

Instructions:

1.In a pot over medium heat, bring enough water to cover sago pearls to a boil. Add sago pearls, stir gently, and cook (for 10 minutes or until translucent), then remove from heat, rinse well and drain.

2.In the pot, add boiled sago and enough cold water to cover. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, and bring to a gentle boil. When water has boiled (for 6 to 10 minutes), then remove from heat, rinse well and drain.

3.In the pot, add enough cold water to cover sago, and bring to a gentle boil, then repeat the process until pearls are tender but chewy and translucent with no white in the center.

4.Under cold, running water, rinse sago a few times to remove excess starch, after allow to cool. Use as needed or store in simple syrup until ready to use.

In a saucepan over medium heat, add water and pandan leaf and bring to a boil for about 2 to 3 minutes. Remove and discard pandan leaf.

Add brown sugar. Stir continuously until sugar is dissolved, then Simmer, stirring continuously, until mixture is slightly thickened and syrupy.

Add cooked sago and continue to cook (for 2 to 3 minutes).

Place tofu block in a cheesecloth-lined steamer and steam (for 7 to 10 minutes or until heated through).

Place tofu in an oven-safe dish, loosely wrap with plastic film, and microwave for about 40 to 50 seconds or until heated through.
